ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder)

Educational content only — not diagnosis. If you’re in crisis, use Get Help.

Quick Overview

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition that can affect attention, impulse control, organization, and activity levels. It can look different from person to person and can impact school and daily responsibilities.

Common Signs

  • Difficulty staying on task
  • Forgetfulness or losing items often
  • Impulsivity or interrupting
  • Restlessness or fidgeting
  • Trouble finishing tasks

Contributing Factors

  • Often runs in families (genetic link)
  • Early development factors (e.g., premature birth)
  • Not caused by laziness or poor character
  • Symptoms can be affected by stress and sleep

Support Options

  • Evaluation by qualified professionals
  • Coaching/strategies for organization and time management
  • CBT skills for planning and impulse control
  • Medication may help for some
  • School supports (504/IEP, accommodations where applicable)
Need urgent support? Visit Get Help for 988 and other resources.

Self-Care Ideas

  • Use planners, timers, and checklists
  • Break tasks into smaller steps
  • Exercise regularly (helps focus)
  • Prioritize sleep consistency
  • Use a clean workspace + reduce distractions

Tip: Start with one small step — consistency matters more than perfection.
